What is Follo?

Decentralized trading protocol

Follo makes the generation and trading of leveraged tokens completely open to anyone. Anyone can build a leveraged token based on any asset with the Follo protocol, or even a leveraged token of the leveraged token itself.

Leveraged AMM

LAMM stands for Leveraged Automated Market Maker, which is based on Structured Funds Management in traditional finance, based on different risk and return characteristics, the fund is separated into two tranch:Priority Fund (BONDS) and Subordinated Fund (PERPETUAL CONTRACTS/LEVERAGED TOKENS).They will represent the interests of LPs and investors.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

A leveraged token has the following common properties.

Underlying Asset: A set of trading pairs, the net value of the leveraged token will be affected by the price of the underlying asset

Unit of denomination: The unit of denomination of the leveraged token should be the same as the underlying pair of the underlying asset, usually DAI or ETH, etc.
Direction: long or short, net value is proportional to the price of the underlying asset when long, and vice versa when short

Leverage: the leverage multiple determines the net value fluctuation, the higher the multiple the greater the net value change is affected by the underlying asset price

The Follo Protocol has three main roles: LP (liquidity provider), Investor, and Liquidator.

LP can add and remove liquidity to obtain a stable additional return, investors can buy and redeem to obtain N times return, and the asset liquidator is responsible for dealing with the assets that need to be liquidated to earn the price difference.

The value of the leveraged token follows the price of the underlying asset, and Follo's smart contract pulls the trading pairs of the oracle machine onchained and mainstream DEX , using their average price as the underlying price.

The handling fee for the leveraged token will consist of three parts, currently:

Subscription fee (0.1% for each transaction), redemption fee (0.1% for each transaction), management fee (0.01% per day).

These fees will be used as an incentive for liquidity providers to add liquidity. At the same time, the management fees for leveraged tokens on centralized exchanges are mostly 10% to 36% per year, compared with 3.65% for Follo at present.

After the leveraged tokens are minted, the leveraged token pool will charge a fee when an investor purchases or redeems a leveraged token, which will be divided equally among the holders based on the pool share (LP token share), and the proceeds will be redeemed when the LP token is removed.

In principle you can have remove your LP token at any time, but the amount removed is limited by the number of remaining subscriptions available in the leveraged token pool.

When an investor purchases a leveraged token, a corresponding percentage of the underlying assets in the pool will be locked, but the unsold portion can be added or removed at any time.
